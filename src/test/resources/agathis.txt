'''''Agathis microstachya''''' (the '''Bull Kauri''') is a [[species]] of [[Pinophyta|conifer]] in the [[Araucariaceae]] family, [[Endemism|endemic]] to [[Australia]]. It was described in 1918 by [[John Frederick Bailey]] and [[Cyril Tenison White]].<ref name=ipni/> It is threatened by [[Habitat destruction|habitat loss]].

==Description==
''A. microstachya'' grows up to about {{Convert|50|m|abbr = on}} in height and {{Convert|2.7|m|abbr = on}} in diameter. The [[Trunk (botany)|trunk]] is unbuttressed, straight and with little taper. Distinctive features are coarse, flaky [[bark]], medium-sized [[Conifer cone|cones]] with 160-210 scales, and [[Leaf|leaves]] with numerous longitudinal, parallel veins.<ref name=Boland/>

==Distribution==
It has a very restricted distribution, being almost limited to the [[Atherton Tableland]] in [[Far North Queensland]], with its elevational range {{Convert|400-900|m|abbr = on}} above [[sea level]].<ref name=Boland>Boland ''et al'', p.38.</ref>

==Timber==
The [[wood]] has an even texture, is easy to work and polishes well. The heartwood is cream to pale brown in colour. It is soft and light with a density of about {{Convert|480|kg/m3|lb/ft3|abbr = on}}. It is not durable in contact with the ground, but can be used for house framing and flooring.<ref name=Boland/>
